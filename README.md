# Digital-Literacy-Course-Project:

Overview of project : 

This project is based on Digital Literacy. This focuses on 5 tasks including creating Canva presentations, creating accounts in various portfolios, creating account in HackerRank and practising coding in that platform, then creating Google Forms and generating responses, writing professional emails to professors for extending assignment date and to internship company for summer internship programs, creating a social media Do’s and Don’ts checklist, making a checklist for preventing cyber crimes and creating a case study for awareness for cyber crimes in today’s digital world.

TASK-1

For this task, I used Canva to design my infographic because it is simple to use and has many ready-made templates. I selected a clean layout and created a presentation based on digital literacy for students. In my design, I included topics like what digital literacy means, some useful digital tools such as Google Docs, GitHub, and LinkedIn, basic safe internet practices, and a few points about email etiquette.

While making the infographic, I found it interesting to explore different icons, colours, and layouts in Canva. At the same time, it was a bit difficult to adjust the layout properly so that writing does not overlap with the design. I had to change font sizes and spacing multiple times to make the design look neat. Overall, this task helped me understand how to present information in a simple and visually appealing way.

TASK-2

For building my professional digital presence, I created and updated profiles on GitHub, LinkedIn, and ResearchGate. TASK-4

One situation where poor digital communication can create problem is when someone sends message without clear explanation. For example, a student sending message to teacher like “I will not submit” without proper reason can sound rude or careless. Because of this, teacher may think that student is not serious about studies. Also in group chats, sometimes people write messages in hurry or anger which can create misunderstanding between friends or team members. Small things can become big issue just because of wrong tone. This problem can be avoided if person think before sending message and use polite words. Writing full sentence with proper reason is important. Also rechecking the message before sending can help to avoid confusion. Good communication is very important in digital platforms for professional and positive environment.

TASK-5

While researching about cybercrime, what surprised me the most is how easily people can get scammed without even realising it. I used to think that only less educated people become victims, but actually even students and smart users also fall into these traps. The methods used by fraudsters are very convincing and sometimes look completely real, which makes it more dangerous.

One habit I will personally change is being more careful while using UPI and online links. Earlier, I did not pay much attention to small details, but now I will always double check before clicking any link or approving any payment request. I will also never share my OTP or personal details with anyone. This awareness is very important in today digital world.
